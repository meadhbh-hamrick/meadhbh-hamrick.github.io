Understanding RISC-V
And this is getting popular enough that I'm putting it up at the top-level page. I spent a couple years working in the RISC-V world. Most of the people who inhabit it are pretty serious computer architecture nerds. A year ago I started writing articles about RISC-V for people who haven't taken Patterson's Computer Architecture class at Berkeley.
Retro-Computing
I like old computers and I sometimes write about them. Looking at old computing hardware and old user experiences gives you an understanding of how we got to where we are. I think it's important to understand this kind of thing.
Strange Programmer Habits
Programmers do the strangest things. This is a series I wrote to highlight some of the weird things I've seen in code along with explanations about why they may not be so weird.
What is VWRAP?
Many years ago I worked for Linden-Lab, makers of Second Life™. As part of my work, we published some of the protocols used to implement that virtual world. Some were published as part of the Virtual Worlds Region Agent Protocol Working Group. I still think the documents we wrote were interesting, even though no one currently uses them. This part of my web page describes the history of the VWRAP working group and what it was trying to accomplish.
